# Edgy Content That Converts

I lost a thirty-thousand-dollar account to a single LinkedIn post in 2024. Same week, the same post pulled nine inbound calls and four became clients. Net positive on the math, net unlucky on the relationship. This is the system I built afterwards: 5 strategic filters, a 5-tier risk spectrum with risk scores, a 100-point pre-post rubric, 5 fill-in-the-blank templates, the backlash recovery playbook, and 50 scroll-stopping hooks you can swipe today.

## What's Inside

- Why polarizing content works in 2026: what Meta does to controversial posts on Facebook (downranks) vs Instagram (more permissive), what X does (often amplifies), and where LinkedIn's tolerance for sharp opinion actually sits. The 'polarize to filter' theory.
- The 5 strategic filters every draft passes through before it leaves your drafts folder: Business Relevance, ICP Alignment, Strategic Purpose, Risk Assessment, Value First. Each filter with a one-line pass/fail check that takes 90 seconds end-to-end.
- The 5-Tier Risk Spectrum: Strategic Personal Vulnerability, Questioning Cultural Norms, Contrarian Industry Declarations, Philosophical Provocations, Direct Audience Challenges. Each tier with a risk score, who it attracts, who it repels, common pitfall, two openers.
- The Pre-Post Risk Scoring Rubric: a 100-point scoring system across 6 categories (Truthfulness, ICP-fit, Recovery viability, Personal stand, Specific value, Originality) with the decision rule. Below 60 do not ship. 80+ ship and prep the recovery thread.
- The 5 Effective Approaches with fill-in-the-blank templates and worked examples: Direct Industry Criticism, Challenging Audience Behaviors, Provocative Industry Positions, Blunt Personal Experiences, Straight-Talk About Results. Five templates. Five examples. Copy and adapt.
- The 5 Approaches to Never Use: Gratuitously Offensive, Politically Charged Without Business Relevance, Personal Attacks on Named Individuals, Oversharing Personal Details, Unverifiable Claims. Each with a wrong example and a corrected rewrite that makes the same argument cleanly.
- The Backlash Recovery Playbook: hour 0 to hour 1 holding pattern, hour 1 to hour 6 decision (clarify, double-down, or pivot), hour 6 to hour 24 response post structure, day 2+ narrative-control move. The doubling-down play and the tactical apology template.
- The Hot Take Hook Vault: 50 scroll-stopping opening lines mapped to the 5 risk tiers (10 per tier) plus 4 platform-specific structural templates showing how to take one hook and reshape it for LinkedIn long-form, X thread, Facebook story-led, and IG carousel.
